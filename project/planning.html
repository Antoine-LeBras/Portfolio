Workforce organisation planning

Where

Paris, France

Work with

PM, CSM and developers

Duration

4 months

Field

UX design, product design

Context & objective

Development of a planning module dedicated to the Ministry of National Education, aimed at assisting secretaries in the management and optimization of the schedules of teachers, substitutes and potential substitutes.

Work

In order to understand the specific challenges of the profession of constituency secretary in the field of primary education, and to meet their needs, expectations and problems, we conducted in-depth interviews with 15 professionals from various constituencies in France. Each interview lasted about an hour, an hour and a half, and the feedback was exceptional, generally in line with the initial brief. However, the crucial points, expectations and problems were identified and highlighted and not present in the basic expectations.

After a thorough analysis, involving iterations on various employee planning and management tools, as well as numerous end-user tests and internal testing, including dozens of simplification tools. We have developed the schedule under development below. It incorporates the essentials necessary to facilitate the tasks of a secretary.

Homepage reworked

Work

The obsolescence of some elements on the homepage led us to redesign it. To do this, we took stock of user requests for important information when using the platform, in order to define the priority information to be found as quickly as possible.

This gave rise to several versions (with more or less detailed information) involving greater or lesser development constraints. In order to decide which version to choose, we carried out tests using tools such as Maze, in order to make progress on other projects while we gathered the information we needed to make this choice.
